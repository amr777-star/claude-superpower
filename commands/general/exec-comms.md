```
Subject: [Company] — [Month/Quarter] [Year] Update

[One sentence — the most important thing this period]

  MRR/ARR: $[X] ([+/-X]% MoM)
  Revenue: $[X] ([+/-X]% vs plan)
  Customers: [X] (net new: [+/-X])
  Burn: $[X]/month
  Runway: [X] months
  Key KPI: [X] ([trend])

WINS:
  ✓ [Win 1 — specific, with numbers]
  ✓ [Win 2]
  ✓ [Win 3]

CHALLENGES:
  ✗ [Challenge 1 — honest, with what you're doing about it]
  ✗ [Challenge 2]

ASKS:
  Can you help with:
  1. [Specific ask — intro to [person/company], advice on [topic]]
  2. [Specific ask]

LOOKING AHEAD:
  Next month we're focused on:
  1. [Priority]
  2. [Priority]

Thanks for your continued support.

[CEO Name]
```

**Investor Update Rules:**
- Send on a consistent schedule (first week of month)
- Be honest about challenges — investors respect transparency
- Always include specific asks — investors want to help
- Keep to 1 page — they read dozens of these
- Bad news first, good news second (builds trust)

### 5. Crisis Communication

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════
CRISIS COMMUNICATION FRAMEWORK
Severity: [P0 Critical / P1 High / P2 Medium]
═══════════════════════════════════════════

STEP 1: ASSESS (first 30 minutes)
  □ What happened? [Factual summary]
  □ Who is affected? [Customers / employees / public]
  □ What's the blast radius? [Number of affected parties]
  □ Is it ongoing? [Contained / spreading]
  □ Legal exposure? [Yes / No / Unknown]
  □ Media aware? [Yes / No / Likely]

STEP 2: INTERNAL ALIGNMENT (first 2 hours)
  □ Crisis team assembled: CEO, Legal, Comms, CTO, affected dept heads
  □ Single source of truth established (shared doc)
  □ Spokesperson designated
  □ Holding statement approved
  □ Employee communication sent: "Here's what happened, here's what we know,
    here's what we're doing. Please direct all inquiries to [person]."

STEP 3: EXTERNAL COMMUNICATION

  TEMPLATE — INITIAL RESPONSE (within 4 hours):
  "We are aware of [issue]. [X customers/users] are affected.
   Our team is actively [investigating/resolving] the situation.
   We will provide updates every [X hours] at [status page/channel].
   We take this seriously and [action being taken]."

  TEMPLATE — FULL RESPONSE (within 24-48 hours):
  "On [date/time], [what happened — factual, no speculation].
   This affected [who and how].

   Root cause: [If known, share. If not: 'Investigation ongoing.']

   What we've done:
   1. [Immediate action taken]
   2. [Customer impact mitigated by]
   3. [Preventive measures implemented]

   What happens next:
   1. [Remediation step with timeline]
   2. [Customer compensation if applicable]
   3. [Systemic fix to prevent recurrence]

   We [apologize/take responsibility]. [Human, empathetic closing.]

   [CEO signature]"

COMMUNICATION HIERARCHY:
  1. Affected customers → Direct email/call, FIRST
  2. All customers → Email within 24h
  3. Employees → Slack/email immediately
  4. Board → Call within 2 hours
  5. Investors → Email within 24h
  6. Media → Reactive only unless proactive is strategic
  7. Social media → Monitor and respond per approved messaging

PRINCIPLES:
  - Speed over perfection — say what you know, acknowledge what you don't
  - Take responsibility — never blame customers or partners
  - Over-communicate internally — employees are your best ambassadors or worst leaks
  - Document everything — crisis comms are legal documents
  - One voice — all external communications through designated spokesperson
```

## Rules
- Board memos: Lead with the headline, not the backstory. Directors are busy.
- Investor updates: Consistency builds trust. Send on schedule even when news is bad.
- All-hands: Energy is contagious. If you're not excited, no one will be.
- Press releases: Journalists read the headline and first paragraph. Everything else is optional.
- Crisis comms: Speed and honesty over polish. Never lie, never speculate, never blame.
- Internal announcements: Assume everything leaks. Write accordingly.
- Every communication should answer: What happened? Why does it matter? What do I do?
- Tone matches audience: formal for board, direct for investors, human for employees
- Always have legal review crisis communications before publishing
