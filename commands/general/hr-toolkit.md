### 1. Job Description

**Structure:**
```
[COMPANY NAME] — [DEPARTMENT]

## [ROLE TITLE]
**Location:** [City / Remote / Hybrid] | **Level:** [IC1-IC6 / M1-M4] | **Reports to:** [Title]

### About [Company]
[2-3 sentences: mission, stage, traction, culture — make it compelling]

### About the Role
[3-4 sentences: why this role exists now, what success looks like, scope of impact]

### What You'll Do
- [Responsibility 1 — outcome-focused, not task-focused]
- [Responsibility 2]
- [Responsibility 3]
- [Responsibility 4]
- [Responsibility 5-8]

### What We're Looking For
**Must-Have:**
- [X] years of experience in [domain]
- [Specific technical skill with measurable criteria]
- [Demonstrated outcome, not just knowledge]

**Nice-to-Have:**
- [Bonus experience]
- [Industry-specific knowledge]
- [Tool expertise]

### Compensation & Benefits
- Base Salary: $[X] - $[Y] (based on level and location)
- Equity: [X-Y]% / [X,XXX-Y,YYY shares]
- Benefits: [Health, dental, vision, 401(k) match, PTO policy]
- Perks: [Remote stipend, learning budget, etc.]

### Our Values
[2-3 company values with brief description]

### Equal Opportunity
[Company] is an equal opportunity employer. We celebrate diversity and
are committed to creating an inclusive environment for all employees.
```

**JD Anti-Patterns to Avoid:**
- "Rockstar/ninja/guru" language (deters diverse candidates)
- >15 requirements (studies show women apply at 100% match, men at 60%)
- Vague requirements ("strong communication") without specifics
- Missing salary range (required by law in CO, CA, WA, NY, and growing)

**Interview Question Bank by Type:**

**Behavioral (STAR: Situation, Task, Action, Result):**
- "Tell me about a time you had to deliver results with an extremely tight deadline"
- "Describe a situation where you disagreed with your manager. How did you handle it?"
- "Give me an example of when you failed. What did you learn?"
- "Tell me about a time you had to influence without authority"

**Technical Assessment:**
- Live coding / system design (engineering)
- Case study with real data (product, business)
- Portfolio review with critique (design)
- Role-play with scenario (sales, CS)

**Culture Fit:**
- "What work environment brings out your best performance?"
- "How do you handle receiving critical feedback?"
- "What's a value or principle you'd never compromise on?"

### 4. Onboarding Plan (30/60/90 Day)

```
EMPLOYEE: ____________  START DATE: ____________  BUDDY: ____________

=== WEEK 1: ORIENTATION ===
□ IT setup complete (laptop, accounts, access)
□ Welcome meeting with manager (expectations, communication norms)
□ Buddy introduction and first lunch
□ HR orientation (benefits, policies, tools)
□ Company overview presentation
□ Meet the team (1:1s with each team member scheduled)
□ Read product documentation and key wikis
□ Dev environment setup (engineering) / Tool access (other)

=== DAY 30: LEARNING ===
Goals:
□ Understand the product deeply (can demo core features)
□ Understand the team's current priorities and OKRs
□ Complete all required training
□ Attend key recurring meetings
□ Ship first small contribution / complete first task
□ 30-day check-in with manager (formal)

Success Criteria:
- Can explain company's product, customers, and competitive position
- Has built relationships with immediate team
- Understands their role's key metrics and expectations

=== DAY 60: CONTRIBUTING ===
Goals:
□ Own and deliver a meaningful project independently
□ Provide input in team planning/roadmap discussions
□ Build cross-functional relationships (2-3 people outside team)
□ Identify one process improvement suggestion
□ 60-day check-in with manager

Success Criteria:
- Delivering work at expected quality without constant guidance
- Asking good questions and proactively seeking information
- Understanding the "why" behind team decisions

=== DAY 90: PERFORMING ===
Goals:
□ Operating at full productivity
□ Contributing to team goals measurably
□ Established go-to expertise area
□ Giving and receiving feedback comfortably
□ 90-day formal review with manager (confirm employment / set first quarter OKRs)

Success Criteria:
- Performance meeting role expectations
- Integrated into team culture and workflows
- Clear on growth path and next-quarter objectives
- Feedback from 3+ colleagues is positive
```

| IC4 | Staff | Multi-project | Organization | Sets direction | 8-12 years |
| IC5 | Principal | Domain-level | Company | Strategic | 12+ years |
| IC6 | Distinguished | Industry-level | Industry | Visionary | 15+ years |

| M2 | Sr. Manager | Multiple teams | Department | Sets priorities | 8+ years |
| M3 | Director | Department | Business unit | Strategy | 10+ years |
| M4 | VP | Function | Company | Executive | 12+ years |

**Salary Band Structure:**
```
Each level has a band: Min (80%) — Mid (100%) — Max (120%)

Placement within band:
  New to level: 80-90% of midpoint
  Proficient: 90-110% of midpoint
  Expert/ready for promotion: 100-120% of midpoint

Annual merit increase budget: typically 3-5% of payroll
Promotion increase: 10-15% or new band midpoint (whichever higher)
Market adjustment: benchmarked annually (Levels.fyi, Pave, Carta)
```

## Rules
- JDs must include salary ranges (legal requirement in growing jurisdictions)
- Interview questions must be job-related and consistent across candidates (legal compliance)
- Performance reviews must be evidence-based — never use subjective language without examples
- PIPs are legal documents — be specific, factual, and maintain the paper trail
- Compensation data should be benchmarked against market (reference sources)
- All HR documents should be reviewed by employment counsel before use
- Equity numbers vary dramatically by stage — always provide ranges, not single numbers
- Onboarding plans should be customized by role, not one-size-fits-all
