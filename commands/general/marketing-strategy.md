```

     Keywords: [primary], [secondary], [long-tail]
  2. [Pillar] — Ties to value prop [Y]
  3. [Pillar] — Ties to pain point [Z]

CONTENT BY FUNNEL STAGE:

  TOFU (Awareness):
    - Blog posts (SEO-driven, 1500-2500 words)
    - Social media thought leadership
    - Industry reports / original research
    - Guest posts on [target publications]
    - Podcasts (guest appearances)
    Goal: Traffic, brand awareness, email subscribers

  MOFU (Consideration):
    - Webinars and workshops
    - Comparison guides ("[Product] vs [Competitor]")
    - Templates and tools (lead magnets)

    Goal: Leads, MQLs, demo requests

  BOFU (Decision):
    - ROI calculators
    - Customer testimonials (video)
    - Product tours / interactive demos
    - Implementation guides
    - Free trial / freemium onboarding content
    Goal: SQLs, opportunities, closed revenue
```

### 4. Email Marketing Framework

```
EMAIL SEQUENCES:

WELCOME SEQUENCE (new sign-ups):
  Email 1 (Day 0): Welcome + quick start guide
  Email 2 (Day 2): Core feature highlight + tutorial
  Email 3 (Day 5): Customer success story
  Email 4 (Day 8): Feature #2 + use case
  Email 5 (Day 12): Social proof + CTA for upgrade/meeting

NURTURE SEQUENCE (MQLs):
  Email 1 (Day 0): Value-first content (not salesy)
  Email 2 (Day 3): Pain point + how we solve it
  Email 3 (Day 7): Case study (similar company/role)
  Email 4 (Day 10): ROI data or comparison guide
  Email 5 (Day 14): Soft CTA — demo/consultation
  Email 6 (Day 21): Final push — limited offer or urgency

RE-ENGAGEMENT (inactive users):
  Email 1 (Day 0): "We miss you" + what's new
  Email 2 (Day 5): Success story from similar user
  Email 3 (Day 12): Special offer or incentive
  Email 4 (Day 20): Final — "Should we close your account?"

EMAIL METRICS BENCHMARKS (SaaS):
  Open rate: 20-30% (good), 30%+ (excellent)
  Click rate: 2-5% (good), 5%+ (excellent)
  Unsubscribe: <0.5% per send
  Reply rate (cold): 5-10% (good), 10%+ (excellent)
  Conversion to meeting: 1-3% of total sends
```

### 6. Paid Media Strategy

```
CHANNEL MIX (B2B SaaS typical):

  Google Ads (30-40% of budget):
    - Search: branded + competitor + high-intent keywords
    - Display: retargeting only (cold display rarely works B2B)
    - YouTube: product demos, testimonials
    Target CPC: $5-20 (varies by industry)
    Target CPA: <$200 for demo request

  LinkedIn (25-35% of budget):
    - Sponsored content: thought leadership + case studies
    - Lead gen forms: gated content offers
    - Retargeting: website visitors + email list matches
    Targeting: Title + Industry + Company size + Seniority
    Target CPC: $8-15
    Target CPL: <$100

  Meta/Instagram (10-20% of budget):
    - Retargeting (high ROI)
    - Lookalike audiences from customer list
    - Brand awareness for broader ICP
    Target CPC: $2-8
    Best for: SMB, self-serve, PLG

  Other:
    - Reddit (community-specific targeting)
    - Twitter/X (tech audience)
    - Sponsorships (newsletters, podcasts)
    - Review sites (G2, Capterra — pay for placement)

BUDGET ALLOCATION FRAMEWORK:
  Test phase: Split evenly, $500-2000/channel
  Scale phase: Shift 60%+ to top-performing channels
  Mature phase: 70% proven channels, 20% optimization, 10% experiments

METRICS BY CHANNEL:
  | Channel | Impressions | Clicks | CTR | CPC | Conversions | CPA | ROAS |
  |---------|------------|--------|-----|-----|-------------|-----|------|
```

## Rules
- Marketing must be measurable — every campaign needs attribution to pipeline
- Positioning comes FIRST — don't start tactics before strategy is clear
- Content should be 70% educational, 30% promotional
- Email is still the highest ROI channel for B2B — invest accordingly
- SEO is a 6-12 month play — start early, measure monthly, expect hockey stick
- Paid media without landing page optimization is wasting money
- CAC payback period matters more than raw CAC — factor in retention
- Always A/B test subject lines, CTAs, and landing pages before scaling spend
