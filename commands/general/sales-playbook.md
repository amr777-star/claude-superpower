### 1. Deal Qualification Frameworks

**MEDDIC (Enterprise, $50K+ ACV):**
```
M — Metrics: What economic impact will the customer achieve?
    "What KPIs will this impact? By how much? Over what timeline?"
    Quantify: "$X saved/earned per year" or "Y hours saved per week"

E — Economic Buyer: Who has budget authority and final sign-off?
    "Who ultimately approves this investment?"
    Must have direct or indirect access. No access = no deal.

D — Decision Criteria: What factors will they evaluate solutions on?
    "What are the top 3 things you need from a solution?"
    Map your strengths to their criteria. Reshape criteria if possible.

D — Decision Process: What are the steps from today to signed contract?
    "Walk me through your typical procurement process"
    Map: Evaluation → Shortlist → POC → Security review → Legal → Sign

I — Identify Pain: What is the specific business pain driving urgency?
    "What happens if you don't solve this in the next 6 months?"
    No pain = no urgency = deal stalls. Quantify the cost of inaction.

C — Champion: Who inside the account is actively selling for you?
    Test: "Would this person take a call from my CEO on a Saturday?"
    Champion criteria: Power, Influence, Access, Motivation (has personal win)
```

**BANT (SMB/Mid-Market, faster qualification):**
```
B — Budget: Is there allocated or can they create budget?
A — Authority: Are you talking to the decision-maker?
N — Need: Is the problem real and acknowledged?
T — Timeline: Is there a compelling event driving urgency?

Scoring: Each dimension 1-3, Total 4-12
  10-12: Hot lead → fast-track
  7-9: Warm → nurture and develop
  4-6: Cold → qualify further or disqualify
```

**SPICED (Modern SaaS framework):**
```
S — Situation: What's their current state and context?
P — Pain: What specific problems are they experiencing?
I — Impact: What's the business impact of the pain (quantified)?
C — Critical Event: What's forcing action now? (Compliance deadline, board mandate, competitive threat)
E — Decision: How do they make decisions? Who's involved?
D — Decision Criteria: What will they evaluate on?
```

### 2. Discovery Call Framework (SPIN Selling)

```
CALL STRUCTURE (45-60 minutes):

OPENING (5 min):
  "Thanks for taking the time. Here's what I'd like to cover today:
   1. Learn about your current situation
   2. Understand your challenges
   3. Explore if there's a fit
   Does that work? Is there anything else you'd like to cover?"

SITUATION QUESTIONS (10 min):
  "How is your team currently handling [process]?"
  "What tools are you using today?"
  "How many people are involved in [workflow]?"
  "What does your current setup look like?"
  Goal: Understand their world. Don't spend too long here.

PROBLEM QUESTIONS (10 min):
  "What's the most frustrating part of [current process]?"
  "Where do you see the biggest bottlenecks?"
  "How much time does your team spend on [manual task]?"
  "What happens when [failure scenario]?"
  Goal: Surface explicit problems they acknowledge.

IMPLICATION QUESTIONS (15 min):
  "When [problem] happens, what impact does that have on [KPI]?"
  "How does that affect your team's ability to [strategic goal]?"
  "What does that cost you in terms of [time/money/risk]?"
  "If this continues for another year, what happens?"
  Goal: Amplify pain. Make the cost of inaction concrete.

NEED-PAYOFF QUESTIONS (5 min):
  "If you could [solve problem], what would that mean for your team?"
  "How would [outcome] impact your [KPI/goal]?"
  "What would it be worth to eliminate [pain]?"
  Goal: Get the buyer to articulate the value of solving the problem.

NEXT STEPS (5 min):
  "Based on what you've shared, I think there's a strong fit. Here's what I suggest:
   [Specific next step with date]. Does [day/time] work?"
  Always end with a SPECIFIC next step. Never "I'll follow up."
```

### 3. Objection Handling Matrix

**Framework: Acknowledge → Isolate → Respond → Confirm**

| Objection | Acknowledge | Response | Evidence |
|-----------|------------|----------|----------|
| **"Too expensive"** | "I understand budget is a concern" | Reframe to ROI: "Customers typically see [X]x return. Let me show the math..." | ROI calculator, case study |
| **"We're happy with current solution"** | "Glad it's working for you" | "Most of our customers felt the same way. What changed was [trigger]. Have you experienced [specific pain]?" | Competitive win story |
| **"Not the right time"** | "Timing matters" | "What would need to change for this to become a priority? What's the cost of waiting [X months]?" | Cost of delay calculation |
| **"Need to talk to my boss"** | "Makes sense" | "I'd love to help you make the case. What questions will they have? Can we include them in our next call?" | Executive summary doc |
| **"We can build it ourselves"** | "Your team is clearly capable" | "Many customers considered that. They found that [build cost + maintenance + opportunity cost] exceeded [our price] by [X]x. Plus you ship [core product] faster." | Build vs buy analysis |
| **"Your competitor has feature X"** | "That's a fair point" | "You're right that [competitor] has [feature]. The question is whether [feature] or [our differentiator] matters more for [their specific goal]." | Battlecard |
| **"We need to see a POC"** | "Absolutely" | "Let's define success criteria first. If [metric] improves by [X]%, what happens next?" | POC framework with committed next steps |
| **"Legal/security review will take months"** | "Understood" | "We've streamlined this. Here's our SOC 2 report, DPA template, and security questionnaire pre-filled. Can we start that process now in parallel?" | Pre-built security package |

### 5. Sales Process & Pipeline

**Stage Definitions:**
```
Stage 0 — PROSPECT (0% probability)
  Entry: ICP match identified
  Activities: Research, outreach sequence
  Exit: Meeting booked

Stage 1 — DISCOVERY (10%)
  Entry: First meeting completed
  Activities: SPIN discovery, stakeholder mapping
  Exit: Pain confirmed, champion identified
  Required: MEDDIC partially filled

Stage 2 — QUALIFICATION (25%)
  Entry: Budget and timeline confirmed
  Activities: Technical deep-dive, demo
  Exit: Decision criteria agreed, process mapped
  Required: Full MEDDIC, economic buyer identified

Stage 3 — EVALUATION (50%)
  Entry: Formal evaluation underway
  Activities: POC/pilot, reference calls, security review
  Exit: Positive evaluation, verbal commitment
  Required: Champion confirmed, competitive position clear

Stage 4 — NEGOTIATION (75%)
  Entry: Selected as preferred vendor
  Activities: Commercial negotiation, legal review
  Exit: Terms agreed
  Required: Redline complete, procurement engaged

Stage 5 — CLOSED WON (100%)
  Entry: Contract signed
  Activities: Handoff to CS/implementation
  Required: Signed contract, PO received

Stage X — CLOSED LOST (0%)
  Required: Loss reason documented, competitive intel captured
```

**Pipeline Metrics:**
```
Pipeline Coverage = Total Pipeline Value / Quota
  Target: 3x for enterprise, 2x for SMB

Pipeline Velocity = (# Opps × Win Rate × Avg Deal Size) / Sales Cycle Days

  Stage 4→5: [X]% (close rate)
```

### 6. Commission & Compensation

**OTE (On-Target Earnings) Structure:**
```
Base:Variable ratio by role:
  SDR: 70:30 (more base, developing pipeline not closing)
  AE (SMB): 50:50 (standard)
  AE (Enterprise): 60:40 (longer cycles, more relationship)
  CSM: 80:20 (retention-focused, less variable)

Commission Models:
  Revenue-based: [X]% of ACV booked
  Quota-based: [X]% of OTE variable per % of quota attained
  Tiered: accelerators above 100% (1.5x at 110%, 2x at 120%)

Accelerator Table:
  0-80% of quota: 0.8x rate (decelerator)
  80-100%: 1.0x rate
  100-110%: 1.5x rate
  110-120%: 2.0x rate
  120%+: 2.5x rate (uncapped or capped at 3x OTE)

SPIFs (Short-term incentives):
  - $[X] per multi-year deal
  - $[X] per new logo
  - [X]% bonus for deals closed before quarter end

Clawback: Commission reclaimed if customer churns within [90] days
```

## Rules
- Discovery is about LISTENING (70/30 rule — listen 70%, talk 30%)
- Never trash competitors — acknowledge strengths, then differentiate
- Every objection is a buying signal — they're engaged enough to push back
- Pipeline without next steps is a graveyard — every opp needs a dated next action
- Compensation drives behavior — design it to reward what you actually want
- Battlecards must be updated monthly — stale intel loses deals
- Win/loss analysis is mandatory — debrief every closed deal within 1 week
